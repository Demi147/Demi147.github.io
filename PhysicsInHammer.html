Trajectory

The calculator below will calculate the distance a hammer will travel with a set of parameters.

  1. Velocity: This is how fast the hammer is going at the point of release.
  2. Angle: The angle of the hammer at point of release.
  3. Initial height: How high the hammer is of the ground at point of release.
Try the following:
  • Velocity: 28m/s
  • Angle: 40 deg
  • Initial height: 1m

Projectile Range Calculator

With the above calculator indicating, max distance traveled is not effected by the mass of the object. That means the if a thrower can throw a 4 kg with the same speed and release angle, the hammer will go just as far as a 7.26kg hammer. A weird concept i know.
The above physics will tell you that the optimal release angle is at 45 degrees, but in practice its hard to achive that angle. In practice the top hammer throwers tend to release between 37 and 42 degrees.

Angular kinematics

Now the term Angular kinematics might sound very scary and complicated, but its just a fancy way in physics to decribe the movement of things that rotate.
So why should a hammer thrower bother with these things? Well. It can explain how much force a hammer will pull on the athlete with, and how fast the athlete should turn to achive optimal release velocity.

  1. Angular velocity

    The first thing we will look at is angular velocity.
    With this calculator we will be looking at the radial velocity specifically. The reason for this is we all ready have the release velocity(V).
    Now we need the radius. In terms of hammer throw its the distance from the centre of the hammer, to the point of rotation. NOTE: the centre of rotation is not the body since the athlete needs to lean backwards to counter act the centrifugal force of the hammer.

    • Velocity (v): 28 m/s
    • Radius (r): 1.76 m

    Hz just means how many times per second something happens. So the output should tell you how fast you should be turning at the point of release.

  2. Centrifugal force

    Centrifugal force is the pulling force the athlete feels when turning with the hammer.
    This is important for 2 reasons.

    1. Understanding research papers. Some research papers on hammer throw talk about "Tension" in the hammer, and ways to increase it. Therefore if you understatnd the force and the way it acts, you can read those papers and understand its meaning better.
    2. Understatnding the amount of force you should feel pulling on you during the throw. If you understand how much force you should feel during the throw you can alter your technique acordinglly.

    Try these values:

    • Mass (m): 7.26 kg (4kg for women)
    • Radius (r): 1.76 m
    • Tangential velocity (v): 28 m/s
